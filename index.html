From Indomalaya to Global Staple

Bananas originated in the Indomalayan realm and Australia, first domesticated in Papua New Guinea. Today, they are grown in at least 135 countries, primarily for their fruit.

The modern "Cavendish" variety dominates the global market, known for its resilience and sweetness. It's a fruit that transcends borders, providing essential nutrition to billions.

  • Botanically considered a large herb, not a tree.
  • The fourth most valuable food crop in the developing world.
  • Naturally radioactive (but completely safe!) due to Potassium-40.
